01. THE CORE PRINCIPLE

Restoring Force Analysis

  • Concept: Force needed to extend/compress a spring scales with distance.
  • Proportionality: Linear relationship between Force (F) and Displacement (x).
  • Negative Sign: Indicates force vector opposes displacement vector.
  • Constraint: Valid only within "Elastic Limit".

02. STRESS & STRAIN METRICS

Technical Definitions

  • Stress (σ): Internal resistance force per unit area.
    [UNIT: Pa (N/m²)]
  • Strain (ε): Dimensional deformation ratio.
    [UNIT: Dimensionless]
  • Young's Modulus (E): Stiffness constant of material.
    E = Stress / Strain

03. MATERIAL BEHAVIOR

Graph Analysis

Plotting mechanical response to applied load.

  • Proportional Limit: Linear region. Hooke's Law applies (σ ∝ ε).
  • Elastic Limit: Threshold for reversible deformation.
  • Yield Point: Onset of plastic (permanent) flow.
  • Fracture Point: Structural failure.

04. DEFORMATION MODES

ELASTIC

Reversible: Material memory retains original geometry.

Ex: Rubber, Spring Steel

PLASTIC

Permanent: Molecular slip causes irreversible set.

Ex: Bent Paperclip, Clay

ELASTIC DEFORMATION

Mechanism:

Transient distortion of crystal lattice. Bonds stretch but do not break.

  • Energy: Stored as Potential Energy (PE).
  • Recovery: 100% Geometry recovery.
  • Region: Occurs before Yield Point.

STATUS: REVERSIBLE

PLASTIC DEFORMATION

Mechanism:

Permanent displacement of atoms (Dislocation Motion).

  • Energy: Dissipated as Heat.
  • Recovery: Non-recoverable geometry.
  • Effect: Work Hardening / Necking.

STATUS: PERMANENT
